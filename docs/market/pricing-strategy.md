# Pricing Strategy

## Working hypothesis

Pricing should stay simple enough for households to understand quickly.

## Considerations

- Free tier for basic household use
- Premium tier later for advanced reports, unlimited customisation, rewards/forfeits, themes, summaries, photo proof, and household insights
- Pricing should be reviewed against current competitors before launch
- Family users may prefer one household subscription
- Shared homes may value low-friction monthly pricing
- International users need clear local currency handling

## Competitor categories

- Family chore apps
- Kids allowance apps
- Family organiser apps
- Habit/task gamification apps

## Competitor examples to track

- S'moresUp
- Greenlight
- GoHenry
- BusyKid
- Cozi
- ChoreBucks
- Other chore chart and family organiser apps discovered during later research

## Pricing rules

- Avoid hardcoded currency assumptions
- Present price in the viewer's locale where possible
- Keep the free or trial story easy to understand
- Treat allowance, reward, and premium pricing as region-sensitive
