# Marketing Plan

## Early channels

- Direct sharing with families and shared homes
- Word of mouth
- Lightweight social proof
- Product-led onboarding and referrals later
- Apple App Store and Google Play listing strategy should be planned

## Social content angles

- TikTok should focus on funny chore arguments, couples, flatmates, family competition, and "who really does the most?" content
- Instagram should focus on reels, carousels, chore memes, app screenshots, family tips, and reward ideas
- Facebook should focus on parenting groups, local groups, family organisation, and household fairness

## Screenshot strategy

- One-tap chore completion
- Leaderboard
- Weekly winner
- Rewards or forfeits
- Fairness dashboard

## Competitor categories

- Family chore apps
- Kids allowance apps
- Family organiser apps
- Habit/task gamification apps

## Competitor examples to track

- S'moresUp
- Greenlight
- GoHenry
- BusyKid
- Cozi
- ChoreBucks
- Other chore chart and family organiser apps discovered during later research

## Messaging themes

- Faster chore completion
- Less nagging
- Fairer points
- Easy household coordination
- Fun and competitive, not strict or corporate
- Visible fairness and household habits
- Weekly summaries people actually want to read

## Content angles for future insight features

- Who really does the most?
- The chore leaderboard never lies
- Couples chore challenge
- Flatmate fairness check
- Weekly household champion
- Sunday reset recap
- The bins were defeated twice this week
- Bathroom duty remains the final boss

## Short-form video ideas

- chore argument before-and-after with a fairness summary
- weekly leaderboard reveal
- couple or flatmate fairness check reaction
- household champion reveal
- funny stats overlay showing the busiest day or most ignored chore
