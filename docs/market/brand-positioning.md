# Brand Positioning

## Positioning statement

Chore Wars is the playful, polished chore app that makes household life feel lighter and fairer.

## Brand traits

- Working name: Chore Wars
- Tone: funny, friendly, playful, clean, polished
- Avoid harsh war-like branding
- Avoid overly childish branding
- Strong enough for international markets

## Market positioning

- Chore Wars should not be only for kids
- It should appeal to families, couples, flatmates, shared houses, students, renters, and young professionals
- It should feel fun and competitive, not like a strict parent or admin tool

## Competitor categories

- Family chore apps
- Kids allowance apps
- Family organiser apps
- Habit/task gamification apps

## Competitor examples to track

- S'moresUp
- Greenlight
- GoHenry
- BusyKid
- Cozi
- ChoreBucks
- Other chore chart and family organiser apps discovered during later research

## Differentiators

- Faster than a typical task app
- More polished than a toy game
- More household-friendly than a solo productivity tool
- Clearer and more competitive than a basic chore chart

## Colour direction

- Warm neutral background
- Strong dark text
- Playful accents such as coral, teal, yellow, mint, and purple
